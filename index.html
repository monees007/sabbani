Inorganic Functional Materials Laboratory

School of Physical Sciences, JNU

Our group is dedicated to the rational design, synthesis, and characterization of novel inorganic and hybrid materials. We bridge fundamental molecular design with real-world applications by creating smart materials with precisely controlled, dynamic, and responsive functionalities to address critical challenges in energy, environment, and technology.

Dr. Supriya Sabbani

Assistant Professor, JNU

Dr. Supriya Sabbani's distinguished career is characterized by a powerful synergy between fundamental inorganic chemistry and applied materials science. Her unique intellectual foundation enables our research group to address complex scientific problems that span the full spectrum from molecular synthesis to functional device development. Her vision is to engineer smart, responsive materials by exploring the rich chemistry of polyoxometalates, coordination complexes, and hybrid frameworks.

Research Programs

Our research programs are highly synergistic, where fundamental discoveries in one area directly inform and enable technological advancements in another. We move seamlessly from molecule to material to application, leveraging deep expertise in synthetic chemistry to create materials with targeted functionalities.

Publications

Our scientific output is documented in a growing portfolio of peer-reviewed articles in high-quality international journals. This body of work reflects our contributions across core research areas, from fundamental studies in solid-state dynamics to the development of applied functional materials.
